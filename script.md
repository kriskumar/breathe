# New guided meditation scripts — for review

Twenty guided meditations to add alongside the current three. Each is narrated
by a voice matched to its mood (🎙 below) via the ElevenLabs API and saved as an
MP3. Pacing — the **0.70× slow speed**, the pauses between lines, and the longer
pause before the closing — is added automatically in processing.

**Voices:** 🎙 Solomon (deep grounding male) · Brad (clear male) · Emily (calm
British female) · Nicole (intimate whisper) · Sahara (velvety female).

Each ends with **"Om shanti, shanti, shanti."**

---

## 1. So Ham — Breath Mantra
*The breath's own sound — "I am that." Fits the app's name.* · ~3 min · `id: so-ham` · 🎙 Sahara

Settle into a comfortable, upright position, and gently close your eyes.
Let your breath move on its own, slow and natural.
As you breathe in, silently hear the sound "So."
As you breathe out, silently hear the sound "Ham."
So... on the inhale.
Ham... on the exhale.
This is the natural sound of the breath, meaning "I am that."
There's nothing to force here. Just listen to So Ham with each breath.
Breathing in, So.
Breathing out, Ham.
If the mind drifts, gently return to the sound.
Let the mantra and the breath become one.
Rest in this quiet rhythm.
When you're ready, release the mantra and sit in the silence it leaves behind.
Gently open your eyes.
Om shanti, shanti, shanti.

## 2. Drifting to Sleep
*A soft body-relaxation to let you fall asleep.* · ~4 min · `id: sleep` · 🎙 Solomon

Lie down comfortably, and let your whole body grow still.
There is nothing to do now but rest.
Feel the weight of your body sinking into the bed beneath you.
Take one long, slow breath in... and let it go with a soft sigh.
Let your feet and legs become heavy and warm.
Let your hips, your belly, and your back soften and release.
Let your hands, your arms, and your shoulders grow heavy.
Let your face soften — your jaw, your cheeks, your eyes.
Your whole body is calm, quiet, and at ease.
With each breath out, you sink a little deeper.
There is nowhere to be, and nothing to hold.
Let your thoughts drift by like clouds, slowly fading.
Allow yourself to drift, soft and safe, toward sleep.
Rest now.
Om shanti, shanti, shanti.

Now bring to mind someone you are thankful for.

Feel how gratitude softens and opens the heart.
Now offer thanks to your own body, for carrying you through each day.
And to your breath, for being with you always.
Let a quiet sense of thankfulness fill you.
Rest here for a moment in gratitude.
When you're ready, carry this feeling with you, and open your eyes.
Om shanti, shanti, shanti.

## 4. The Mountain
*Grounding and stability — becoming steady and unmoving.* · ~3 min · `id: mountain` · 🎙 Solomon

Sit comfortably, with your spine tall and your shoulders relaxed.
Imagine a great mountain, still and silent, rising before you.
Its base is wide, rooted deep into the earth.
Its peak is calm, high above, touching the sky.
Now let yourself become the mountain.
Your body is the mountain — grounded, solid, and unmoving.
Seasons pass, storms come and go, but the mountain remains.
Thoughts and feelings are like passing weather, drifting across the sky.
You do not need to chase them, or push them away.
You are the mountain, steady beneath it all.
Breathe, and feel your own quiet strength.
Rest in this stillness, rooted and calm.
When you're ready, let the image dissolve, keeping its steadiness.
Gently open your eyes.
Om shanti, shanti, shanti.

## 5. Calm in Anxiety
*Settling the nervous system when worry rises.* · ~3 min · `id: calm-anxiety` · 🎙 Emily

Find a comfortable position, and let your eyes close if that feels safe.
Notice that, right now, in this moment, you are okay.
Take a slow breath in through the nose... and a long breath out.
Let the exhale be longer than the inhale, releasing the tension.
Place a hand on your chest or belly, and feel it rise and fall.
You don't need to fix anything right now. Just breathe.
If anxious thoughts arise, notice them, and let them pass.
They are only thoughts — they cannot harm you here.
Breathe in calm... and breathe out worry.
With each slow breath, your body grows a little safer.
Feel your feet on the ground, steady and supported.
Stay here, breathing gently, as the wave of feeling softens.
You are safe. You are here. You are okay.
When you're ready, open your eyes.
Om shanti, shanti, shanti.

## 11. Focus & Concentration

Take a few breaths to arrive, leaving the busyness behind.
Choose a single anchor — the feeling of the breath at the tip of the nose.
Rest your full attention there, on each breath in, and each breath out.
When the mind wanders, that's natural. Simply notice, and return.
Each time you return, your focus grows a little stronger.
There is only this breath, this moment, this one point of attention.
Let everything else fade quietly into the background.
Steady and clear, like a calm flame that does not flicker.
If a thought pulls you away, gently guide your attention back.
Stay with the breath, collected and present.

## 12. Five Senses Grounding
*A 5-4-3-2-1 practice to return to the present when overwhelmed.* · ~3 min · `id: grounding-senses` · 🎙 Emily

Sit comfortably, and take a slow, steadying breath.
This practice gently brings you back to the present, through your senses.
Notice five things you can see around you. Let your eyes rest softly on each one.
Now notice four things you can feel — your feet on the floor, the air, your clothes, your seat.
Now listen for three things you can hear, near or far.
Now notice two things you can smell, or simply the feeling of the air as you breathe.
And one thing you can taste, or the quiet space inside your mouth.
Take a slow breath, feeling more present, more here.
You have arrived, fully, in this moment.
Rest for a moment in this grounded awareness.
When you're ready, carry this presence with you.
Om shanti, shanti, shanti.

Breathe, and make a little room around the feeling.
Notice where you feel it in your body — the chest, the throat, the belly.
Hold it with curiosity, the way you might hold a frightened child.
Offer it kindness. It's okay. I'm here. This will pass.
Feelings are like weather — they arise, and they move through.
Rest, letting the feeling soften in its own time.
When you're ready, gently open your eyes.
