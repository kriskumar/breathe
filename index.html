❓ How to use
  1. Pick a pranayama from the dropdown. The short note below it tells you what the breath is for; some (like Bhramari) add a tip on how to do it.
  2. Choose a level — Beginner, Medium, or Advanced. Higher levels run more cycles or longer breaths.
  3. Press Start. Follow the circle: it grows as you inhale, holds while you hold, and shrinks as you exhale. The number counts down each phase.
  4. Optional cues: turn on 🔊 voice guidance to hear each phase, or 🔔 to chime at the end of every cycle. Toggle them off for a silent, visual-only session. Reset returns to the start.
  5. 🧘 Meditate tab: switch tabs at the top for guided sessions — play a recording (like the Aum chant) or follow a spoken meditation. Tap any item to start, and Stop to end it.

Tip: keep the breath gentle and never strain — ease off if you feel light-headed. Works offline once loaded.

📚 The research behind the breaths

These patterns aren't arbitrary — most map onto breathing techniques studied for their effects on the nervous system, heart rate variability (HRV), mood, and sleep. The recurring finding: slow breathing with a longer exhale shifts you toward "rest and digest."

  • Coherent (5-5, ~6 breaths/min) — the most-studied "resonance" rate for raising HRV. Steffen 2017, Noble & Hochman 2019
  • Extended exhale (Clear Mind, Relax 1–3) — a longer out-breath raises vagal HRV on its own. Bae 2021
  • 4-7-8 (Relax 2) — popular sleep aid; improves sleep quality with daily practice. Mualifah 2026
  • Box (4-4-4-4) — "tactical breathing" for focus and composure under pressure. Jones 2026
  • Bhramari (humming) — humming breath raises HRV and parasympathetic tone. Latha 2022
